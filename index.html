Ceremonies & Participants

Keep a note of who did what. The next time the lodge performs that ceremony, add those brethren as contributors at that time. You are allowed to record multiple people for one piece of work.
The date will be recorded against the later ceremony, but their Ceremonial Record will be updated accordingly.

Whilst the fact the Obligation was done, must be completed for each candidate - you only need to add the remaining ceremony parts and participants for one of the candidates. There is no need to do it for every candidate.

Tracing boards delivered outside of a ceremony are not part of the Book of Constitutions or a ceremony, and thus cannot be listed as a Hermes agenda item — nor can the participant be directly credited.

For a full tracing board, you may add it on to the participants list for the next 1st or 2nd Degree ceremony the lodge performs. The date will be wrong, but the deliverer's Ceremonial Record will be updated.

The same principle applies to Royal Arch lectures, demonstrations of degrees, and working tools delivered outside of ceremony. Record them at the next appropriate ceremony and add the brother as a contributor.

The participant to record on Hermes is the brother presenting the certificate — not the brother receiving it.

These items are not part of business governed by the Book of Constitutions and therefore have no drop-down entry in Hermes. They should of course be recorded in your minute book as is your custom.
If you wish to credit a brother for delivering an item which is a usual part of a degree ceremony, add it on to the participants list at the next appropriate ceremony (initiation, passing, raising, or exaltation).

Members & Honorary Members

You are not able to add a Non-subscribing member of your lodge to Hermes, for election to Honorary Membership, as additional checks are needed not visible at your lodge level.
Add the item to your summons and ensure Rule 167 is correctly valid. After the ballot, let PGReg know and he will add the brother to your lodge as an honorary member.

Add the item to the Pre-Meeting Agenda and ballot for him. There is no need for a Notice of Motion — just put it on the summons, issued in good time. It must be a physical ballot with a ballot box.
There is no need for a member to resign before he is made an Honorary Member. By accepting Honorary Membership, he is deemed to have resigned as a subscribing member on acceptance.
Be very careful that the honorary member understands: if he is not subscribing to any other lodge, he may become an "unattached member" and will only be able to visit other lodges once per lodge, ever. If he is in the Royal Arch, he will be automatically excluded from that after one year.

Please add them to the count for Visitors -as they are not a member when the meeting opens, which is when the Tyler's book is completed.

Check whether there is a lodge-specific email address attached to the Office of WM (or Treasurer), or whether another brother's email address has been added to this office.

Annual Returns & Billing

There is one Annual Return for UGLE and one for Province. These are found on two "tabs" next to each other. Please remember to check and approve on both tabs.
A common oversight is approving only one tab. Both must be completed separately.

There is now one annual bill covering dues for each member who was a member in the preceding year — even if they resigned or died during that year.
The number on your Annual Return will equal: current members + resignations + exclusions + deaths.
Any registration fees are also included on this single annual bill. There is no need to pay separately when each candidate is initiated during the year.

You will receive emails at both your personal email address and the secretaries office email address (if one exists) — for both UGLE and Province. That means up to 4 emails in total (2 for Province, 2 for UGLE).
You will receive one set approximately one month before the year-end to prompt you to check resignations and exclusions are up to date, then another set a month later to approve the Return and send it to the Treasurer.

The "Send to Treasurer" button only appears the day after the end of your financial year — for example, on 1st September if your year-end is 31st August.
This is because dispensations or registration fees from the final days of the year may still be added to the bill until midnight on the last day.
In the meantime, use the time to check all your members are correct — update any that need excluding for unpaid subscriptions, or who have resigned.

Meetings & Dispensations

Use Dispensation under Lodge Management — for emergnecy meetings or to change the date of an exisitng meeting.
If the date change is permitted by the BoC (e.g. the meeting falls on a public holiday or an excluded day such as Good Friday), completing a dispensation request is the easiest way to move the date and let the Provincial Office know the new date (there is no charge).

Use "Add Meeting" under Lodge Management. This will be approved by PGSec. Note there is no concept of an "Emergency Meeting" in Royal Arch - no dispensation is needed — extra meetings can be called by the Principals at any time.

Lunar or irregular lodges only may use "Add Meeting Date" from the Lodge Management screen.
All other lodges must use "Move by Dispensation" or "Emergency Meeting by Dispensation." Do not use "Add Meeting Date" if your lodge has regular, fixed meeting dates - or you wish to add an Emergency meeting. It will not be approved.

The BoC requires that the Tyler must be elected, and this must appear on the summons each year.
If the lodge wishes to appoint a brother from within the lodge, a Proposition duly seconded is made in open lodge: "I propose that the WM appoints the Tyler from among the brethren at the Installation." A show of hands carries this.
Only external Tylers need to be put on the Election Meeting agenda on Hermes.
A lodge must have a named Tyler - it is one of the Required Offices. It is also the first question asked during the opening of a lodge. Lodges must be Tyled.

Other Topics

Recording charity donations on Hermes is optional.
If the donation is to your lodge relief chest, you can ignore it altogether — statistics are collected when money is disbursed from there. For a donation to a local or unregistered charity (e.g. a Scout group), click "Charity Not Found" and enter it manually.
Money for Almoners (widows' Christmas presents, flowers for the hospitalised, bereavement cards, etc.) is not charitable giving and should not be recorded here.
If you can't find a charity by the search box -use "charity not found" and type in the best guess. A further reminder that recording these donations is optional and the Meeting Report can be filed without completing it.
